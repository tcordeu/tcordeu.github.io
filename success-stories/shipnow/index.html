    How I helped Shipnow

    Shipnow is an Argentinean startup and, since its inception in 2014, it has simplified the shipment process of more than 300 clients. Shipnow takes the responsibility away from sellers of having to handle complex shipments across the whole country for each of their products. Through a simple yet fully featured web application and seamless integrations all major e-commerce platforms, clients can take their minds off shipment intricacies and focus on what they do best: selling.

    As Shipnow grew they realized that there current architecture was not holding to the standards they wanted. Although they where familiar with Docker and, in fact, they used it for the majority of there projects, they did not know about Kubernetes. So, I helped them migrate all the infrastructure to Kubernetes.

    Architecture#

    Shipnow needed an architecture that could handle a high traffic load while also optimizing the resources needed and through Kubernetes I managed to deliver. Not only the new architecture is able to handle three times more load but I also managed to reduce the monthly cost by about 20%.

    Monitoring and Alerting#

    One of the most emphasized topics that came up when discussing the project with Shipnow was: we need to know when something is wrong and we need to know it fast. With the help of Prometheus and the EKL stack (Elasticsearch, Kibana and Logstash) I managed to provide fast and detailed alerts for the whole infrastructure while also giving developers more tools to solve difficult bugs.

    CI/CD#

    Through key abstractions provided by Kubernetes and the addition of new self-hosted runners, developers of Shipnow are now able to deploy new code with just one click and the guarantee that there will be zero downtime on each deployment.

    Infrastructure as Code#

    Before the migration changes to the infrastructure where hard and frightening because there was not clarity on what was being done. After the migration, with the help of Terraform and Ansible, every part of the infrastructure is present in source code and every change happens through a commit. This guarantees that everything is documented and that every change is recorded … no more “magic”.
